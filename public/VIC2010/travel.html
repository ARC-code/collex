Getting to Charlottesville and U.Va.

The University of Virginia is located in Charlottesville, which stands at the junction of Interstate 64 and Route 29 in Central Virginia. The area is served by the Charlottesville-Albemarle Airport and Richmond Airport.

For complete driving directions to the University, and information about parking on Grounds, please visit the U.Va. website.

Local Attractions

Charlottesville is an area rich in history and culture. The Corner is the heartbeat of the University community and just a short walk from University Grounds. Downtown Charlottesville is a short bus ride away and features even more shopping, local restaurants and night life.

Looking for a little taste of Virginia history? You could start with a with a tour of the Rotunda and Thomas Jefferson’s Academical Village and if that’s not enough TJ for you, visit his home at Monticello.

It’s not all about Jefferson though. Charlottesville and the surrounding county provide a wealth of possibilities for tourists interested in history, vineyards, outdoor adventure and more. Here are a few places to get started.
