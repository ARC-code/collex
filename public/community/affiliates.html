ACKNOWLEDGEMENTS

Our work has been supported by the generosity of the Andrew W. Mellon Foundation, which awarded NINES initiator Jerome McGann a Mellon Distinguished Achivement Award in 2002 and generously extended its funding for a further two years of work, ending in 2008. Mellon monies were chiefly being used to fund ARP (a group for Applied Research in 'Patacriticism), which developed NINES metadata standards, tools, and interfaces.

NINES is currently supported by an initiative from the President of the University of Virginia, John Casteen, and receives funding from the President's Office, the College of Arts and Sciences, and the Office of the Vice President and Chief Information Officer, James Hilton. Additional support is provided by the Department of English at the University of Virginia.

Recognition is also due to The University of Virginia Library's Digital Research and Scholarship team for all of the institutional and technological support they provide for the NINES project. Learn more about digital scholarship initiatives at the University of Virginia here.
