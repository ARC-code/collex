COLLEX:
search, browse, collect, and annotate NINES resources online now!

 

NINES is supported by a highly-skilled research and development team based at the University of Virginia. This group is responsible for producing the tools and interfaces described here.

Erik Hatcher brings a content-management mindset to the ARP team, as well as expertise in web application development and search engine technologies. He co-authored two books, Java Development with Ant and Lucene in Action, and has written several articles.

Duane Gran is the Project Manager for ARP. He advises developers on software design and formalizes requirements for new software for various academic stake holders at UVA and in NINES.

Textual scholar William J. Hughes, project manager of the Rossetti Archive, is a doctoral candidate at the University of Virginia.

Nick Laiacona comes to ARP from the computer game industry. At Kesmai Corporation he produced, designed and programmed massively multiplayer online games. His games have been published on America Online, Earthlink, and Game Storm. Mr. Laiacona received a BA in Computer Science from Drew University in 1994.

Dr. Jerome McGann is John Stewart Bryan University Professor at the University of Virginia.

Dr. Bethany Nowviskie is a the senior researcher at ARP. She conceived and designed the Collex tool that powers NINES. Nowviskie also serves as Design Editor of the Rossetti Archive and is a founding member of SpecLab.

Dr. Carl Stahmer, who is contributing supplemental software to NINES, is acting associate director of MITH at the University of Maryland.
