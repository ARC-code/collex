NINES is actively soliciting the contribution of scholarly material to its federated archive. Potential contributors should contact the heads of our Victorian, Romantic, and Americanist editorial boards with technical or procedural questions: victorian@nines.org ; romantic@nines.org ; american@nines.org.

Guidelines for new projects: The NINES editorial boards are currently compiling interface and markup guidelines. These will be made available on this site as soon as possible. In the meantime, NINES strongly encourages contributors to encode their texts in XML (rather than HTML) and in accordance with widely-accepted TEI standards for markup. That said, TEI markup is not a prerequisite for participation in NINES. Attention to markup standards in the early phases of your project will simply facilitate the production and formatting of necessary metadata for inclusion in NINES.

We are happy to discuss technical issues with scholars just beginning a project of digitization and with those who need to retro-fit their existing electronic resources to conform with and take advantage of markup standards.

The MLA's "Guidelines for Editors of Scholarly Editions" is a valuable resource.
