COLLEX:
search, browse, collect, and annotate NINES resources online now!

 

Collex is an open-source collections- and exhibits-builder designed to aid humanities scholars working in digital collections or within federated research environments like NINES. Collex operates under the assumption that the best paths through a complex digital resource are those forged by use and interpretation. A Collex approach works to assist scholars in recording, sharing, and building on the interpretive purposes to which they put their online teaching and research environments. Collex leverages current developments in folksonomy and semantic-web technology to perform data mining operations and enhance knowledge discovery, leading scholars and students to see connections among digital objects, based on the contexts into which those objects have been placed (implicitly or explicitly) by past scholarly activity in the system.

Users of Collex in NINES can:

  • collect, tag, analyze, and annotate trusted objects (digital texts and images vetted for scholarly integrity);
  • reorganize and publish objects in fresh critical perspectives;
  • share these new collections with students and colleagues, in a variety of output formats;
  • and, without any special technical training, produce interlinked online and print exhibits using a set of professional design templates.
A detailed description of Collex is available here, and Collex forms the backbone of the NINES pilot project, available for use here. A Collex website and blog is also available, as is an outside review.
